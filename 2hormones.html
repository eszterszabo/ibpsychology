Functions of Hormones


The hormons we produce (or take) can have serious effects on our physical development as well as our behaviour. It is commonly known that men and women produce many different hormones, however , for the sake of simplicity we will only look at hormones that both sexes produce, regardless of the quantities produced by each sex, and the amount that is needed for each sex to feel the effect of the hormones.
The IB requires psychology students to know the functions of TWO hormones, with studies for each.


Cortisol

Cortisol is a stress hormone that is relased by our body when we haveangative or to any extent stressful experiences. It can be released in different quantities, but a prolonged exposure to it, especially to large amounts will have negative effects on the body. These negaive effects may include sleeplessness, sweating, poor concentration or memory problems.

RESEARCH:

Martinez and Kessner, 1991 - They wanted to see how the activity levels of the neurotransmitter acetylchlorine (ACh) would effect the memory of mice, as they had to go trough a maze several times. The 3 groups of mice had they ACh performace decreased, left as normal and increased. They found that those mice whos ACh activity was increased performed the best, and those whose was decreased perforemd the worst.


Testosterone

Testosterone is a stress hormone that is produced by the testeicles in men and by the ovaries in women. It is a hormone that can stimulate hair growth and lead to increased levels of aggression, which is what psychological studies mostly concentrate on.


RESEARCH:

Dabbs et al, 1995 - Wanted to investigate the relations between testosterone levels and crimes committed by inmates. For the experiment, the testosterone levels of 692 male prisoners were measured. It was found that those who committed sexual crimes had much higher levels of this hormone than those who committed property crimes.
