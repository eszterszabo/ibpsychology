Genetic Inheritance


When looking at genetic inheritance, it is best to look at studies that either involve parents and their children, siblings or monozygotic twins (MZT). MZTs are the most used in this type of research because as their genes are fully identical, their other behavioural attributes can easily be compared and related back to their genetic inheritance.
Through genetic traits behaviours such as aggression, nurturing behaviour in mothers, or intelligence (based on IQ levels) are areas that are often studied.


RESEARCH:

Bouchard et al, 1990: The Minnesota twin study - This study aimed to look at how much the environment, and how much genetics are responsible for the IQ levels of twins.
For this, Bouchard looked at over 100 pairs of identical and non-identical twins, who were raised either apart and together. Approximately 50 hours were spent on the testing of twins by researchers.
Bouchard concluded that approximately 70% of intelligence can be attributed to genetics, and 30% of it can be attributed to environmental factors.


Suomi ans Lesch, 1996 -The aim of this study was to see if monkeys´ ability to process serotonin had an effect on their levels of aggression.
For this, first, Suomi ans Lesch identified the gene that was responsible for the processing of serotonin in juvenile monkeys. Then they looked at whether there was a correlation between the monkeys´ abilities to process serotonin ( by taking samples from their cerebral spinal fluids), and the levels of aggression that they displayed.
They found a strong correlation between the monkeys´ inability to process serotonin and their heightened levels of aggressive behaviour.
